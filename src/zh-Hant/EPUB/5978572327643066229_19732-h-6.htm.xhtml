WITH A FRIGHTENED CRY, SHE WAS FOLDED TO HIS BREAST.

IX

It was ten days later. Rossi had surrendered to Parliament, but Parliament had declined to order his arrest. Then he had called for the liberation of Roma, but Roma had neither been liberated nor removed. "It will not be necessary," was the report of the doctor at the Castle to the officers of the Prefetura. The great liberator and remover was on his way.

這是十天後的事了。羅西已向國會投降，但國會並未下令逮捕他。然後，他呼籲解放羅馬，但羅馬既沒有被解放，也沒有被移除。「不需要」這是城堡的醫生向縣政府的官員報告的內容。偉大的解放者和撤走者正在路上。

At Rossi's request Dr. Fedi had been called in, and he had diagnosed the case exactly. Roma was suffering from an internal disease, which was probably hereditary, but certainly incurable. Strain and anxiety had developed it earlier in life than usual, but in any case it must have come.

根據羅西的要求，他們叫來了費迪博士，他精確地診斷了病情。羅馬正在受到一種內部疾病的折磨，這種疾病可能是遺傳的，但確實是無法治癒的。壓力和焦慮使它比平常的生活早些發展，但無論如何，它最終都必定會出現。

At first Rossi rebelled with all his soul and strength. To go through this long and fierce fight with life, and to come out victorious, and then, when all seemed to promise peace and a kind of tempered happiness, to be met by Death—the unconquerable, the inevitable—it was terrible, it was awful!

最初，羅西用他所有的靈魂和力量反抗。經歷這場持久而激烈的生命之鬥，並勝利出線，然後，當一切似乎承諾和平和某種淡定的幸福，卻被死亡——那無法征服，那不可避免的迎面而來 ——這實在是可怕，太糟糕了！

He called in specialists; talked of a change of air; even brought himself, when he was far enough away from Roma, to the length of suggesting an operation. The doctors shook their heads. At last he bowed his own head. His bride-wife must leave him. He must live on without her.

他召集了專家們；提議換個環境；甚至當他離羅馬足夠遠時，他提出進行手術。醫生們搖了搖頭。最後他低下了自己的頭。他的新娘必須離開他。他必須沒有她繼續生活。

Meantime Roma was cheerful, and at moments even gay. Her gaiety was heart-breaking. Blinding bouts of headache were her besetting trouble, but only by the moist red eyes did any one know anything about that. When people asked her how she felt, she told them whatever she thought they wished to hear. It brought a look of relief to their faces, and that made her very happy.

與此同時，羅馬感到心情愉快，有時甚至有點歡樂。她的快活讓人心碎。她困擾的是眼花痛的頭痛，但只有從濕潤的紅眼睛裡，人們才能知道這件事情。當人們問她感覺如何時，她告訴他們她認為他們希望聽到的任何事情。這讓他們的臉上浮現出一絲的舒解，這讓她非常快樂。

With Rossi, during these ten days, she had carried on the fiction that she was getting better. This was to break the news to him, and he on his part, to break the news to her, had pretended to believe the story. They made Elena help the little artifice, and even engaged the doctors in their mutual deception.

"在這十天裡，她和羅西一直製造了一種她正在康復的假象。這是為了告訴他消息，他也為了告訴她消息，假裝相信了這個故事。他們讓埃倫娜幫助這個小把戲，甚至騙了醫生，製造了他們彼此的欺瞞。"

"And how is my darling to-day?"

"今天我的寶貝過得如何？"

"Splendid! There's really nothing to do with me. It's true I have suffered. That's why I look so pale. But I'm better now. Elena will tell you how well I slept last night. Didn't I sleep well, Elena? Elena.... Poor Elena is going a little deaf and doesn't always speak when she is spoken to. But I'm all right, David. In fact, I'll feel no pain at all before long, and then I shall be well."

"太好了！這與我無關。的確，我有過痛苦。這就是我看起來如此蒼白的原因。但我現在好多了。艾琳娜會告訴你我昨晚睡得有多好。我沒有睡得很好嗎，艾琳娜？艾琳娜...可憐的艾琳娜有點耳背，不總是在被人說話時回話。但我很好，大衛。事實上，過不了多久我就不會感到任何疼痛，然後我就會康復了。"

"Yes, dear, you'll feel no pain at all before long, and then you'll be well."

"是的，親愛的，不久你就不會有任何疼痛，然後你會恢復健康。"

It was pitiful. All their words seemed to be laden with double meanings. They could find none that were not.

這真是悲哀。他們的所有話語都似乎充滿了雙重含義。 他們找不到任何沒有的。

But the time had come when Roma resolved she must speak plainly. Rossi had lifted her into the loggia. He did so every day, carrying her, not on his arm as a woman carries a child, but against his breast, as a man carries his wife when he loves her. She always put her arms around his neck, pretending it was necessary for her safety, and when he had laid her gently in the bed-chair she pulled down his head and kissed him. The two little journeys were the delight of the day to Roma, but to Rossi they were a deepening trouble.

然而，當羅馬決定必須清楚地說明時候來了。羅西每天都把她抬到陽臺。他不是像女人抱小孩一樣抱著她，而是像一個愛她的男人抱著他的妻子一樣，把她緊緊地貼在胸口。她總是假裝十分需要他，將手臂環繞在他的脖子上，當他把她輕輕地放在躺椅上後，她拉下他的頭親吻他。這兩次小旅行對羅馬來說是一天中的喜悅，但對羅西來說卻愈發困憂。

It was the sweetest day of the sweet Roman spring, and Roma wore a light tea-gown with a coil of white silk about her head such as is seen in the portraits of Beatrice Cenci. The golden complexion was quite gone, there was a hard line along the cheek, a deep shadow under the chin, the nostrils were pinched and the mouth was drawn. But the large eyes, though heavy with pain, were full of joy. They did not weep any more, for all their tears were shed, and the light of another world was reflected in their depths.

那是甜蜜的羅馬春天中最甜蜜的一天，羅馬身穿輕便的茶色連衣裙，頭上繞著一圈白絲，就像在貝阿特麗切·切奇的肖像中看到的那樣。金黃的膚色已經完全消失，臉頰上有一條硬硬的線，下巴下有深深的陰影，鼻孔收縮，嘴巴掉了下來。但那雙大眼睛，儘管充滿痛苦，卻充滿了喜悅。他們不再哭泣，因為所有的淚水都已經流完，而另一個世界的光芒則反映在他們的眼睛裡。

Rossi sat by her side, and she took one of his hands and held it on her lap between both her own. Sometimes she looked at him and then she smiled. She, who had lost him for a little while, had got him back at last. It was only just in time. A little break, and they would continue this—there. Ah, she was very happy!

羅西坐在她的身邊，她拿起他的一隻手，用兩隻手握在她的膝蓋上。有時她看著他，然後微微一笑。她，暫時失去他，最後又得到他。這正好及時。稍微休息一下，然後他們會在那裡繼續這——在那裡。啊，她非常快樂！

Rossi's free hand was supporting his head, and he was trying to look another way. Do what he would to conquer it, the spirit of rebellion was rising in his heart again. "O God, is this just? Is this right?"

"羅西的空手撐著他的頭，他試圖朝另一個方向看。不管他怎麼努力去戰勝它，叛逆的精神又再次在他的心中升起。「上帝啊，這是公正的嗎？這是對的嗎？」"

They were alone on the loggia. Above was the cloudless blue sky, below was the city, hardly seen or heard.

他們獨自一人在陽臺上。上面是無雲的藍天，下面是城市，幾乎看不見也聽不見。

"David," she began, in a faint voice.

"大衛，"她微弱地開始說。

"Dearest?"

"最親愛的?"

"I have been so happy in having you with me again that there is something I have forgotten to tell you."

"我有你在我身邊再次給我帶來的快樂實在太大了，我忘了告訴你一件事。"

"What is it, dear?"

"親愛的，有什麼事嗎？"

"Promise me you will not be shocked or startled."

"承諾我，你不會感到驚訝或驚嚇。"

"What is it, dearest?" he repeated, although he knew too well.

"他重複說：“什麼事，親愛的？”儘管他已經太清楚了。

"It is nothing.... Yes, hold my hands tight. So!... Really it's nothing. And yet it is everything. It is ... it is death."

"沒什麼... 是的，緊緊握住我的手。這樣!... 真的不是什麼。而且它是一切。它是... 它是死亡。"

"Roma!"

"羅馬!"

Her eyelids trembled, but she tried to laugh.

她的眼皮在顫抖，但她試圖笑出來。

"Yes, dear. True! Not immediately. Oh, no! not immediately. But signed and sealed, you know, and not to be put aside that anybody may be happy much longer."

"是的，親愛的。真的！並非立刻。哦，不，並非立刻。 但已經簽署並密封，你知道的，不應該被擱置，讓任何人都能再快樂些許久。"

She was laughing almost gaily. But all the same she was watching him closely, and now that her word was spoken she suddenly became conscious of a secret desire which she had not suspected. She wanted him to contradict her, to tell her she was quite wrong, to convince and defeat her.

她幾乎是歡笑的樣子。但即便如此，她仍然密切地關注著他，現在她的話說出口，她突然認識到了一種她以前未曾察覺的秘密渴望。她希望他反駁她，告訴她她完全錯誤，說服並打敗她。

"Poor little me! Pity, isn't it? It would have been so sweet to go on a little longer—especially after this reconciliation. And when one has kept one's heart under bolt and bar so long...."

"可憐的小我！可憐，不是嗎？如果能再繼續一段時間 ——尤其是在這次和解之後，那該多好。當一個人把自己的心閉上鎖那麼久...."

Her sad gaiety was breaking down. "But it's better so, isn't it?"

她悲傷的歡樂正在崩潰。“但這樣不是更好嗎？”

He did not reply.

他沒有回答。

"Ah, yes, it's better so when you come to think of it."

"啊，是的，當你開始思考時，這樣確實更好。"

"It's terrible!" said Rossi.

"這太可怕了！"羅西說。

"Don't say that. It's a thing of every day. Here, there, everywhere. God wouldn't allow it to go on if it were terrible."

"不要那麼說。 這種情況每天都會發生。這裡，那裡，到處都是。如果這是可怕的，上帝不會讓它繼續下去。"

"It's bitterly cruel for all that."

"這實在是殘酷到了極點。"

"Not so cruel as life. Not nearly. For instance, if I lived you would have to put me away, and that would be harder to bear than death—far harder."

"並不像生活那麼殘酷。遠遠不及。例如，如果我繼續活著，你將必須將我送走，那會比死亡更難承受 —— 遠比死亡更難。"

"My darling! What are you saying?"

"我的寶貝！你在說什麼？"

"It's true, dear. You know it's true. God can forgive a woman even if she's a sinner, but the world can't if she's only a victim of sin. It's part of the cruelty of things, but there's no use repining."

"這是真的，親愛的，你知道這是真的。儘管一個女人是個罪人，神可以原諒她，然而如果她只是罪惡的受害者，這個世界卻不能。這就是事物殘酷的一部分，但沒有什麼用去抱怨。"

"Roma," said Rossi, "I take God to witness that if that were all that stood between us nothing and nobody should separate you and me. I should tell the world that you had every virtue and every heroism, and without you I could do nothing."

"羅馬，"羅西說，"我以神為證，如果那就是我們之間的全部差距，沒有什麼與誰可以將你我分開。我將告訴世界，你具有每一種美德和每一種英勇，沒有你，我什麼也做不了。"

Her eyes filled with a fresh joy.

她的眼睛充滿了新的喜悅。

"You set me too high still, dear. Yet you know that I was far too small and weak for your great work. That was why I failed you at the end. It wasn't my fault that I betrayed you..."

"你還是把我看得太高了，親愛的。然而你知道，我對你的巨大工作來說太渺小、太弱了。這就是為什麼我最後無法達到你的期望。我背叛你並非我意願..."

"Don't speak of my betrayal. I thank God for it, and see now that it was the best that could have happened."

"不要提及我的背叛。我感謝上帝賜予它給我，並且現在看到，那是最好的事情發生了。"

She closed her eyes. "Is it your own voice, dearest? Really yours? Hush! I shall wake and the dream will pass."

她閉上了眼睛。 "這是你自己的聲音，最親愛的嗎? 真的是你的嗎? 保持安靜! 我會醒來，夢想會過去的。"

A little jet from his heart of flame burst out in spite of his warning brain, and he was carried away for the moment.

"他心中的小小火焰射出一道亮光，儘管他的理智一再提醒他，但他還是被那一刻的情感所帶走。"

"My poor darling, you must get well for my sake. You must think of nothing but getting well. Then we'll go away somewhere—to Switzerland, as you said in your letter. Or perhaps to England, where you were born, and where your father lived his years of exile. Dear old England! Motherland of liberty! I'll show you all the places."

"我可愛的寶貝，為了我你必須康復。你只需要專心康復。然後我們會去某個地方，像你在信中說的瑞士，或者你出生的地方英國，你的父親在那裡度過了他的流亡生活。親愛的舊英格蘭！自由的母國！我會帶你參觀所有的地方。"

She was dizzy with the beautiful vision.

她為美麗的視覺效果所眩暈。

"Oh, if I could only go on like this for ever! But I mustn't listen to you, dearest. It's no use, you know. Now, is it?"

"哦，如果我能永遠這樣下去該有多好！但是，我不能聽你的，親愛的。你知道，這沒有用。現在，是這樣嗎？"

The spirit which had exalted him for a moment took flight, and his heart rose into his throat.

那股曾讓他短暫振奮的精神消逝了，他的心漲到了喉嚨。

"Now, is it?" she repeated.

"現在是嗎?"她重複說。

He did not answer, and she dropped back with a sigh. Ah, it was cruel fencing. Every word was a sword, and it was cutting a hundred ways.

他沒有回答，她嘆了口氣退了回去。啊，這真是殘酷的劍舞。每個詞都是一把劍，它正在無數個方向上切割。

At that moment a band of music passed down the street. Roma, who loved bands of music, asked Rossi to lift her up that she might look at it. A little drummer boy was marching at the head of a procession, gaily rolling his rataplan.

在那一刻，一支樂隊走過街道。羅馬愛好樂隊，她請求羅西把她舉起來，這樣她就能看到樂隊。一個小鼓手男孩高興地在隊伍前頭行進，輕快地滾動著他的連手鼓。

"He reminds me of little Joseph," she said, and she laughed heartily. Strange mystery of life that robs death of all its terrors!

"他讓我想起了小約瑟夫,"她說，並開懷大笑。 奇妙的生命之謎，使死亡失去了所有的恐怖！

He put his arm about her to support her as they stood by the parapet, and this brought a new tremor of affection, as well as a little of the old physical thrill and a world of fond and tender memories. She looked into his eyes, he looked into hers; they both looked across to Trinità de' Monti, and in the eye-asking between them she said plainly, "Do you remember—over there?"

他在欄杆旁支撐著她，他的手臂環紮住她，這令到他們之間沸騰著新的愛意，也帶來一絲原始的身體壯烈，以及充滿溫情和細膩回憶的世界。她深深地看著他的眼睛，他也緊緊地凝視著她的眼睛；他們同時望向Trinità de' Monti，然後在他們的眼神交流中，她清楚地問道，“你還記得在那邊的事情嗎？”

Roma was assisted back to the bed-chair, and then, conversation being impossible, Rossi began to read. Every day he had read something. Roma had made the selections. They were always about the great lovers—Francesca and Paolo, Dante and Beatrice, even Alfred de Musset and poor John Keats, with the skull cap which burnt his brain. To-day it was Roma's favourite poem:

羅馬在進行了協助後，回到了床椅，然後，因為無法進行對話，羅西開始朗讀。他每天都會讀一些東西。羅馬選了這些讀物。這些總是關於偉大的愛人——弗朗切斯卡和保羅，但丁和比阿特麗斯，甚至阿爾弗雷德·德·穆塞和可憐的約翰·濟慈，他的頭蓋帽燒傷了他的大腦。今天是羅馬最喜歡的詩：

"Teach me, only teach, Love!
As I ought
I will speak thy speech, Love,
Think thy thought...."

"教我，只是教我，愛！ 如我所應該的， 我會講你的語言，愛， 思考你的思考...."

His right hand held the book. His left was between Roma's hands, lying blue-veined in her lap. She was looking out on the sunlit city as if taking a last farewell of it. He stopped to stroke her glossy black hair and she reached up to his lips and kissed them. Then she closed her eyes to listen. His voice rose and swelled with the ocean of his love, and he felt as if he were pouring his life into her frail body.

他的右手拿著書，左手則臥在羅馬的手中，碧藍的血管在她的膝蓋上畫出線條。她看著陽光照亮的城市，彷彿在與它道別。他停下來輕撫她光澤的黑髮，她伸手親吻了他的嘴唇。然後，她閉上眼睛聆聽。他的聲音如洪流般湧出他的愛，他感覺自己就像是將生命注入她脆弱的身體中。

"Meet, if thou require it,
Both demands,
Laying flesh and spirit
In thy hands."

"如果你需要， 滿足這兩個要求， 將肉體和靈魂 交到你的手中。"

Her blanched lips moved. She took a deep breath and made a faint cry. He rose softly, and bent over her with a trembling heart. Her breathing seemed to have ceased. Had sleep overtaken her? Or had the tender flame expired?

她白蒼的嘴唇動了起來。她深深地吸了一口氣，發出了微弱的哭聲。他柔和地起身，帶著顫抖的心臟俯身看她。她的呼吸似乎已經停止了。是睡意襲來了？還是那溫柔的火焰熄滅了？

"Roma!"

"羅馬!"

She opened her eyes and smiled.

她張開眼睛並露出笑容。

"Not yet, dear—soon," she said.

"還不行，親愛的，很快就會了," 她說。

THE END

這部小說的主角是一位農夫，他擁有誠懇無華的人生觀。他失去了妻子，家庭由一系列能力各異的家庭助手照顧，直到一位來與最不可能的來源的年輕女子出現，她不僅成為他的妻子，還贏得了他的尊重，最終贏得了他的愛情。這是一場光明細膩的戀愛，展現了雙方逾越所有困難，抵擋朋友的譴責和敵人痛 bitterness 的愛情。

SAUL OF TARSUS. By Elizabeth Miller. Illustrated by André Castaigne.

這個故事的場景設在耶路撒冷、亞歷山大、羅馬和大馬士革。使徒保羅、殉道者史蒂芬、黑落德·阿格里帕和皇帝提比留與卡利古拉都是書中的重要角色。精彩的描述和最純淨、最高尚的愛情故事，使這個宗教浪漫故事變得格外引人注目。
